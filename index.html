Brief Bio

I'm a PhD Student in the CompleX Lab, University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC), under the supervision of Prof. Tao Zhou. Between 2016.08 and 2017.09, I was a Visiting Student at the Collective Learning Group at the MIT Media Lab, Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), hosted by Prof. Cesar A. Hidalgo. Between 2012.09 and 2014.06, I was a Master Student in School of Computer Science, UESTC. In 2012, I received the Bachelor of Science degree from School of Mathematical Sciences, UESTC.

I'm working in the emerging field named Computational Socioeconomics. My research interests focus on understanding phenomena in social and economic systems through empirical and theoretical analysis. I am interested in solving problems related to Network Science (network models, information spreading, vertex similarity), Computational Social Sciences (knowledge diffusion, reputation ranking, economic status inference), and Data Analysis (social media, online rating, co-authorship).

Please view my publication profile on Google Scholar, Scopus, Research ID and my review records on Publons or directly download my CV.
